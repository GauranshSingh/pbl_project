Process Digital Twin for Business Process Monitoring

To design and implement a Process Digital Twin prototype that reconstructs and analyzes business process execution over time using structured event-log data (case_id, activity, timestamp), inspired by Celonis-style process mining.

Problem Statement

Organizations execute thousands of process instances daily (e.g., order-to-delivery). However, raw system logs do not provide insight into process delays, bottlenecks, incomplete executions, or variant behavior. A Process Digital Twin is required to reconstruct real-time process state and generate analytical summaries for monitoring and improvement.

Literature Review

Process Mining has emerged as a powerful data-driven technique for discovering, monitoring, and improving real-world processes using event logs. Tools such as Celonis demonstrate how structured logs (case_id, activity, timestamp) can be transformed into process models, performance analytics, and bottleneck detection. The concept of Digital Twins has traditionally been applied to physical systems in manufacturing and IoT environments, where sensor data is used to replicate the real-time behavior of machines or assets. Recent academic work suggests extending the Digital Twin paradigm beyond physical systems toward business processes. However, most implementations either focus on predictive machine learning models or static dashboards. There exists a need for a structured, explainable Process Digital Twin prototype that reconstructs process evolution at the case level and provides interpretable analytics such as completion rates, delay detection, and variant analysis.

Research Gap

This project adapts the Digital Twin concept to business process systems using event-log reconstruction and case-level state modeling.
